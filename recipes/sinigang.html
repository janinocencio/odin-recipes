Sinigang

Description

Make no mistake about it: this killer pork sinigang is to die for. I mean no exaggeration when I say that there are few dishes Filipinos love more than this tart and satisfying stew. Chock full of vegetables and hearty meat, this dish satisfies the soul –– and the stomach! Zesty and refreshing, pork sinigang is a comfort food for the ages. Just the familiar smell of this warm soup is enough to put a smile on your face; what more actually eating it!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly (cubed)
  • 66 grams Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix
  • 2 pcs eggplant (sliced)
  • 8 pcs okra
  • 18 pcs string beans (cut in 2-inch pieces)
  • 5 ounces daikon radish
  • 1 bunch kangkong or spinach
  • 1 pc onion (wedged)
  • 2 pcs tomato (wedged)
  • 6 pieces shishito pepper
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a cooking pot. Saute onion until layers separate. Add half of the tomato. Saute for 2 minutes.
  2. Add pork belly. Continue sauteeing until the pork browns while adding around 1 tablespoon fish sauce.
  3. Pour water. Cover the pot and let the liquid boil.
  4. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc with Gabi. Cover and adjust heat between low to medium. Cook for 30 minutes or until the pork gets tender.
  5. Add radish. Cover and continue cooking for 30 minutes.
  6. Add eggplant, string beans, and okra. Cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add kangkong stalks and remaining tomato. Cook for 3 minutes.
  8. Season with fish sauce (as needed) and ground black pepper.
